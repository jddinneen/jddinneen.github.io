BWP3 Information and Society

Prof. Dinneen • Winter Semester 2021/22

Description

In this course students learn about the background and current landscape of the information society. In doing so, we will examine the role of information and technology in shaping today’s society from various international, social, societal, and ethical perspectives, and will analyse relevant factors in politics, economy, and regulation. Upon completion of the course, students will have both a broad and deep understanding of the various requirements and desiderata of the information society will be able to discuss how these requirements are met by information institutions and research.

Delivery Modes and Languages

This year (2021) the course will transition back to in-person meetings: Zoom will be used in the first week (VL & HS) to discuss the requirements for meeting in person (3G & Mund- und Nasenschutz.), and from week 2 onwards we will meet in person so long as it is safe to do so (i.e. we will return to online teaching via Zoom if necessary). The Zoom link & room number are on the course’s Moodle page.

BWP3 consists of seminars (HS) on Tuesday afternoons and lectures (VL) on Wednesday afternoons, each starting at 12:15 (ending by 13:45). The VL consist primarily of lectures with integrated class discussion, whereas the HS consist of student presentations and various discussions, some of which require students to prepare in advance. Students are expected to attend all meetings.

Language requirements

The course will be conducted primarily in English. Please note that students’ varied linguistic backgrounds, skills, and challenges have been considered in the design and delivery of course materials and in the review of assessments; generally, linguistic performance will not be assessed! Students are therefore encouraged to participate in English as much as possible. The MAP may be submitted in English or German.

VL Participation

Students should attend and participate in lectures. VL participation is pass/fail: respectfully participating in the class discussions for the majority of the weeks will earn the Leistungspunkte (LP).

HS Participation

HS participation is comprised of student presentations and discussion, including some discussion that requires written preparation. If asynchronous presentation is necessitated by health or other issues, students should contact the instructor in advance. All HS participation is pass/fail: presenting & participating according to the basic requirements will earn the LP.

  • Discussion in the HS meetings introduces new course themes or reviews the results of discussion preparation. Students are expected to attend and discuss, and in some cases, will be required to prepare something to share with the class (e.g. summary of readings).
  • Presentations should cover a narrow idea that fits within the VL from the week prior (e.g. a presentation on data ethics comes after VL5, in HS6), and according to the student’s interests (e.g. professional, scholarly, personal interests). Each presentation should last max. 20 minutes (a script may be used), and contain the content enumerated below. Students are required to attend each others’ presentations and participate in the discussions. The presentation schedule will be decided at the start of the semester. Note: it is permissible – and smart – to write the MAP essay on the same topic as your presentation! Presentations should contain:
  1. a very brief introduction
  2. a summary of relevant information found in scholarly literature and reputable press
  3. a clear takeaway (i.e. conclusion – what did you learn?)
  4. three open-ended questions to stimulate class discussion.

MAP Fachartikel

The MAP Fachartikel is an essay with original synthesis and argumentation using suitable evidence and reasoning to support a coherent and novel thesis about a topic covered in the module. After a brief introduction the author should deeply analyse the chosen topic, give support for their thesis, and then – importantly – end by discussing what, if anything, the LIS field can do to have an impact on the topic (broadly construed). The essay should be ca. 6.000 to 10,000 characters (excluding spaces) and therefore ca. 3 to 5 pages, in APA7 style, in English or German.

Readings and Resources

Readings and resources will be provided on Moodle prior to each week. In addition to assigned readings, students are expected to select (according to their interests) and read 2-3 supplementary readings each week from the course bibliography.
