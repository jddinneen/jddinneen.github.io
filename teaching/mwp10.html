MWP10 Information Governance und Informationsethik

Prof. Dinneen • Summer Semester 2021

Description

In this course students learn about the background and current landscape of information governance and information ethics. In doing so we will reflect on the role of information and technology in shaping today’s society from various social, societal, and ethical perspectives, and will analyse relevant regulatory and economic factors. Upon completion of the course, students will have both a broad and deep understanding of the need for and development of information governance and information ethics and will be able to discuss how these ideas are implemented in information institutions and research.

Delivery Modes and Languages

MWP10 consists of seminars (HS) on Tuesday afternoons and lectures (VL) on Thursday afternoons, each starting at 12:15 and ending by 13:45. This year the course will be conducted via synchronous online delivery, but includes flexibility for asynchronous study as needed. One link to the Zoom session for all VL & HS is available on the course Moodle page. The VL consist primarily of lectures with some integrated class discussion, whereas the HS consist of student presentations and various discussions (details below). Recordings of the VL will be provided, but students are expected to attend both VL & HS.

Language requirements

The course will be conducted primarily in English. Please note that students’ varied linguistic backgrounds, skills, and challenges have been considered in the design and delivery of course materials and in the review of assessments; generally, linguistic performance will not be assessed! Students are therefore encouraged to participate in English as much as possible. All assignments may be submitted in English or German.

VL Participation

Students should attend and participate in lectures. If necessary, students may instead watch the lecture recordings and post text comments to extend the class discussion. Live discussion and posted comments must be on topic, respectful, and coherent. VL participation is pass/fail, i.e. discussing the VL content (live or via posts) according to the requirements and for the majority of the weeks will earn the Leistungspunkte (LP).

HS Participation

HS participation is comprised of discussion, student presentations, and written assignments. Students should attend and participate in seminars synchronously (i.e. live). If asynchronous presentation is necessitated by health or other issues, students should contact the instructor in advance. All HS participation is pass/fail, i.e. presenting, participating, and submitting assignments according to the basic requirements will earn the LP.

  • Discussion in the HS meetings serves to introduce new course themes or present/review the results of short assignments. Students are expected to attend and discuss, and in the case of short assignments, to prepare to summarise their results.
  • Presentations should briefly introduce one narrow topic from the VL delivered the week prior (e.g. a presentation on data ethics comes after VL5), and then further relevant information according to the student’s interests (e.g. professional, scholarly, personal interests) with reference to relevant scholarly literature and/or reputable press. Presentations should last max. 20 minutes (a script may be used) and end with (a) a clear conclusion and (b) 3 open-ended questions to stimulate class discussion. Students are therefore expected to attend others’ presentations and participate in the discussions. The presentation schedule will be decided at the start of the semester. Note: it is permissible – and even strategic – to write the MAP essay on the same topic as the presentation!

MAP Essay

The MAP Hausarbeit is an essay with original synthesis and argumentation using suitable evidence and reasoning to support a coherent and novel thesis about a topic covered in the module. After a brief introduction the author should deeply analyse the chosen topic, give support for their thesis, and then end by discussing what, if anything, the LIS field can do to have an impact on the topic (broadly construed). The essay should be ca. 40.000 characters (excluding spaces) and therefore ca. 20 pages, in APA6 style, in English or German.

Readings and Resources

Readings and resources will be provided on Moodle prior to each week. In addition to assigned readings, students are expected to select (according to their interests) and read 2-3 supplementary readings each week from the course bibliography.
